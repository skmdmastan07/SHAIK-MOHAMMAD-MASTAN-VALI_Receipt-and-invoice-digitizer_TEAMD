DAY 11 – AI MODELS

AI Models:
AI models are programs that are trained and pre-trained for specific tasks or use cases.

Examples:
Chatbot
Image generation
Video generation
Prediction systems


--------------------------------------------------

Platforms / Companies Providing AI Models:

Google – Gemini
OpenAI – GPT models
Groq – LLM models
Microsoft – Copilot
Microsoft Azure – Azure OpenAI Service


--------------------------------------------------

Production Ready or Not:

Some AI models are used for testing and learning.
Some models are production-ready and used in real-world applications like chatbots, automation, and analytics.


--------------------------------------------------

LLM (Large Language Model):

LLM is trained on large amounts of text data.
It understands and generates human-like language.

NLP:
NLP (Natural Language Processing) is mainly used for text-related tasks like text classification.
LLM is more advanced and can handle multiple types of data (text, image, etc.) depending on the model.


--------------------------------------------------

AI Platforms:

Google AI Studio
OpenAI Platform
Groq Platform

These platforms allow developers to use AI models through APIs.


--------------------------------------------------

Rate and Token Limit:

Rate – How many requests you can send in a given time.
Token Limit – Maximum number of words/characters the model can process in one request.

More tokens = longer input and output.


--------------------------------------------------

Chat Completion Model Example:

response = client.chat.completions.create(
    model="gpt-4o-mini",
    messages=messages,
    temperature=0.7,
    max_tokens=500
)


--------------------------------------------------

Temperature:

Range: 0.0 to 1.0

Low temperature (0.0 – 0.3):
More accurate and less creative output.

Medium temperature (0.5 – 0.7):
Balanced creativity and accuracy.

High temperature (0.8 – 1.0):
More creative and random responses.
